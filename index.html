Doomscrolling & Ethical Digital Engagement

Balancing Business Metrics with Consumer Well-being

Marketing Insight

Doomscrolling drives engagement but harms users.
Platforms benefit from increased CTR and session length, while users suffer from anxiety, fatigue, and attention loss.

Behavioral Science Findings

Anxiety, sleep disruption, and cognitive overload are common consequences of doomscrolling behavior. Long-term exposure leads to reduced trust and platform disengagement.

Strategic Design Response

  • Introduce fatigue-aware UX elements ("Take a break" prompts)
  • Reframe algorithmic goals around emotional well-being
  • Explore emotionally intelligent AI interfaces like IO
  • Shift KPIs toward "well-being per session"

Marketing Ethics

Should platforms optimize for time-on-site at the cost of mental health? Marketers have a role in reshaping this dynamic. Ethical design builds long-term loyalty.

Analytics Opportunity

Platforms can implement metrics like:

  • Digital Fatigue Index
  • Emotional Friction Scores
  • Churn Risk via Behavioral Exhaustion
